About Me

I am a Senior Mechanical Design Engineer with over 10 years of experience across defence, avionics, RF systems, consumer electronics, automotive, and construction technology sectors. I hold an MSc in Mechatronics Embedded Systems from the University of Southern Denmark (SDU) and a CSWP certification in SolidWorks.

My work covers the full product development cycle — from concept and CAD design through FEA and CFD simulation, DFM and DFA, IP-rated enclosure design, thermal management, and electromechanical documentation. I have led and contributed to over 20 R&D programmes, with a strong focus on precision engineering and delivering robust, manufacturable designs.

Projects & Work

IFF Transponder and Interrogator

Designed a modular CNC-milled aluminium chassis for an airborne IFF system, integrating transmitter, receiver, power supply, and digital cards within tight space and weight constraints. Slot-fitting PCB cards with wedge locks and integrated airflow channels ensured thermal performance and serviceability. Qualified through environmental testing and entered production at 100 units.

High Power Amplifier Chassis

Designed CNC-milled aluminium chassis with integrated heat sinks for a high-power RF amplifier system. Cooling fin geometry was optimised through coupled CFD and FEA thermal simulation to meet dissipation targets under worst-case load. RF isolation between stages was maintained through coordinated internal module layout. Thermal targets were met and the design entered production at 50 units.

Mobile RF Amplifier Unit

Designed a large sheet metal enclosure achieving IP54 ingress protection and managing electronics temperatures across an operating range of minus 30 to plus 40 degrees Celsius. A forced cooling system with blowers and air ducts directed airflow across internal fins. Anti-vibration isolators attenuated transport shock loads. FEM analysis validated structural integrity prior to field deployment.

Precision Magnetic Flux Test Bench

Designed a precision test bench for magnetic flux measurement of permanent magnet motor rotors, commissioned by SECOP as part of an MSc thesis at SDU. Non-magnetic materials were used throughout to eliminate measurement interference. Self-aligning cone mounts ensured rotor concentricity, micrometer-driven linear stages achieved 0.01mm sensor positioning resolution, and smooth rotation at 2 RPM was maintained without speed variation.

Excavator Sensor Mounting System

Designed aluminium sheet metal mounting structures for sensor, camera, and antenna systems on excavators, engineered for outdoor durability with UV and chemical resistant surface treatments. Fool-proof assembly features including asymmetric geometry and keying ensured correct field installation without engineering support. Design entered production with 55 units delivered and a 45-unit follow-on order in progress.

Automotive Sensor and Perception Mounting

Designed sheet metal and injection-moulded mounting brackets for camera, radar, and LiDAR systems on automotive platforms. Packaging studies identified mounting positions satisfying field of view and keep-out zone requirements within existing vehicle geometry. Extensive 3D printing enabled rapid iteration during R&D, with physical vehicle testing used to validate sensor orientation and clearances before production design was finalised.
